Mr. Zhang, age 45, with a spouse (43) and two children (10 and 14), aims to retire at 55, fully fund both children's college educations, optimize taxes, and implement estate planning. With $800,000 household income and $5.00M in liquid assets, the family is in a strong position.

The base plan projects a retirement portfolio of $11.70M at age 55 and a 92% probability of funding retirement through age 93 (target ≥85% ✓). The plan is sensitive to retiring earlier than 55 or a sharp market drop at retirement (both fall to ~77%), so a 2–3 year cash buffer is recommended. Education is fully fundable via 529 contributions of $77,427/yr. The most material planning gap is estate tax: if the TCJA exemption sunsets to ~$7M/person, projected estate tax rises to $6.93M — making annual gifting and trust structures essential.

6. Tax Optimization

  • Max tax-deferred & Roth: Contribute $47,000/yr to 401(k)s; use backdoor Roth IRA ($14,000/yr) since income exceeds Roth eligibility.
  • Roth conversions (ages 55–61): The 7 low-income years before SS are ideal to convert ~$120,000/yr from pre-tax to Roth, filling the 22–24% brackets. This cuts future RMDs and estate value.
  • Asset location: Hold bonds in tax-deferred accounts, equities in taxable/Roth. Use municipal bonds for tax-free income in taxable.
  • Tax-loss harvesting: Realize net capital losses in the brokerage (offset up to $3k ordinary + carryforward) to lower taxable gains.
  • 529 state deduction: If resident in a deduction state, capture the state income-tax benefit on 529 contributions.
  • Charitable: After 73, use Qualified Charitable Distributions from IRAs and a Donor-Advised Fund for bunching.

7. Estate Planning

With the portfolio continuing to grow in retirement, the projected estate reaches $31.33M by age 93 (peak ~$32.67M). Under current law the federal exemption (~$13.99M/person, portable to $27.98M/couple) leaves a modest $1.34M tax. However, if the TCJA exemption sunsets to ~$7M/person, estate tax jumps to $6.93M.

  • Revocable Living Trust — avoid probate, ensure seamless succession for spouse and children.
  • Annual gifting — use the $19,000/donor/recipient exclusion now:张先生 + spouse → 2 children = up to $76,000/yr out of the estate.
  • 529 as estate tool — front-load (superfund 5-yr) up to $95,000/child; removes assets from estate while funding education.
  • Pour-over Will & beneficiary review — align retirement, 529, and insurance beneficiaries with the trust.
  • Life insurance — consider a policy to cover any estate-tax liquidity need and income replacement; review term coverage for both spouses.
  • Long-term care — evaluate LTC insurance or a dedicated funding reserve (the plan absorbs a $300k LTC event with minimal impact).
